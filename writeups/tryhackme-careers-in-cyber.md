Roles Covered

🔵 Security Analyst

Monitors and protects an organisation's networks. Works with stakeholders to understand security requirements, compiles reports on network safety, and develops security plans based on emerging threats.

Skills: SIEM tools, log analysis, reporting, stakeholder communication

Path: SOC Level 1

🔵 Security Engineer

Designs and maintains the security infrastructure — firewalls, controls, and systems — to prevent attacks. Works with threat and vulnerability data to build and test security solutions.

Skills: Networking, system administration, scripting, cloud security

Path: Cyber Defense / Red Teaming

🔵 Incident Responder

Identifies and mitigates attacks as they unfold in real time. Develops incident response plans and protocols, and produces post-incident reports. Measured by MTTD, MTTA, and MTTR metrics.

Skills: Digital forensics, malware analysis, threat intelligence, pressure management

Path: SOC Level 1 / Threat Hunting

🔵 Digital Forensics Examiner

Investigates breaches and cybercrimes by collecting and analysing digital evidence — file systems, memory, logs, and network captures — while adhering to legal procedures.

Skills: Evidence handling, forensic tools, report writing, legal awareness

Path: SOC Level 1

🔴 Malware Analyst

Analyses malicious software through static and dynamic methods to understand its behaviour, impact, and origin. Supports incident response and threat intelligence teams.

Skills: Reverse engineering, sandbox analysis, assembly basics, scripting

Path: Malware Analysis modules

🔴 Penetration Tester

Simulates attacks against systems and applications to find vulnerabilities before real attackers do. Produces detailed reports with actionable remediation steps.

Skills: Kali Linux, Burp Suite, Metasploit, scripting, report writing

Path: Jr Penetration Tester / Offensive Pentesting

🔴 Red Teamer

Goes beyond penetration testing — emulates a full threat actor operation over weeks, testing an organisation's detection and response capability as well as its defences. Usually external to the organisation.

Skills: Advanced exploitation, evasion, persistence, physical security, social engineering

Path: Red Teaming



What I Learned

This room gave me a clearer picture of where I could realistically fit within the cybersecurity industry. The distinction between penetration testing and red teaming was something I hadn't fully understood before — red teamers are testing the whole security operation, not just the technical defences. It also reinforced that defensive roles are just as technical as offensive ones, and that roles like incident response and forensics suit people who think analytically and work well under pressure.



Challenges

No technical challenges — this is a reading and comprehension room. The main value is in reflecting on which role genuinely appeals and mapping that to a concrete learning path.



Takeaways



Cybersecurity is not one career — it's a broad industry with highly specialised roles

Both red and blue team paths are well-supported on TryHackMe with structured learning paths

Soft skills like communication, documentation, and working under pressure are emphasised across almost every role

Penetration testing and red teaming are related but distinct — scope, duration, and objectives differ significantly
